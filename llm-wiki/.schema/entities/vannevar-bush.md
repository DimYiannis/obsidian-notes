## Overview

American engineer and science administrator (1890–1974). Director of the US Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII. Author of "As We May Think" (The Atlantic, 1945) — the essay that introduced the [[memex]] concept and laid the intellectual foundation for hypertext and personal knowledge management.

## Key Facts

- "As We May Think" (1945): seminal essay describing the Memex
- Proposed associative trails as the organizing principle for personal knowledge — links reflecting chains of thought, not hierarchical filing
- Vision: private, curated, actively maintained personal knowledge store
- Limitation of his era: no automated maintenance — his Memex required the human to build all trails manually
