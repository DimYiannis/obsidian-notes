## Definition

A hypothetical personal knowledge device conceived by [[vannevar-bush]] in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." A mechanized desk that stores books, records, and communications and can retrieve them via associative trails — links between documents reflecting the user's own chains of thought.

## Key Properties

- **Personal and private**: individual's curated store, not a public library
- **Associative linking**: connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves — anticipates hypertext by decades
- **Trail-based navigation**: user builds and saves associative trails through material; trails shareable with others
- **Never realized**: technology of 1945 (microfilm, levers) couldn't deliver the vision

## Connections

- [[vannevar-bush]] — inventor and author of the concept
- [[llm-wiki-pattern]] — direct spiritual descendant; solves the maintenance problem Bush identified but couldn't automate. The wiki's cross-references are the Memex's associative trails; the LLM is the maintenance engine Bush lacked.
