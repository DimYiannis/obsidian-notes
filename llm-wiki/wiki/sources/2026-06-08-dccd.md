## Summary

Reddy et al. (2025) identify a flaw in standard [[constrained-decoding]]: applying hard constraints at every token step imposes a "projection tax" — the model is forced toward valid but potentially semantically wrong tokens when its probability mass falls mostly on invalid ones. [[draft-conditioned-decoding]] (DCCD) fixes this with a two-step procedure: generate an unconstrained draft first (semantic planning), then apply constrained decoding conditioned on that draft (form enforcement). Draft conditioning increases feasible probability mass before constraints apply, reducing distortion.

## Key Points

- **Projection tax**: standard constrained decoding distorts generation when valid tokens have low model probability — form is enforced at the cost of meaning
- **DCCD two-step**: (1) unconstrained draft — model plans freely; (2) constrained generation conditioned on draft — form enforced on a semantically coherent plan
- **Why it helps**: conditioning on the draft shifts probability mass toward valid tokens before constraints apply → smaller projection tax → better semantic + structural output
- **Best-of-K**: generate K unconstrained drafts, select best, apply constrained pass — further quality improvement at K× inference cost
- **Result**: GSM8K, 1B model — **15.2% → 39.0%** (+24pp); small model matches much larger constrained baselines
- **Training-free**: no weight changes; works as inference-time wrapper around any LLM
