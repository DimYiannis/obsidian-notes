# Draft-Conditioned Constrained Decoding for Structured Generation in LLMs

*Source: arxiv.org/abs/2603.03305. Reddy et al. (2025). Ingested 2026-06-08. Immutable.*

Authors: Avinash Reddy, Thayne T. Walker, James S. Ide, Amrit Singh Bedi

Abstract: A single syntax error can make LLM output unusable. Traditional constrained decoding enforces validity but can distort generation when the model assigns low probability mass to valid continuations. DCCD is a two-step training-free inference procedure: generate an unconstrained draft, then apply constrained decoding conditioned on that draft. Draft conditioning increases feasible mass and reduces the cumulative projection tax from hard constraints. Optional best-of-K draft selection further improves performance. GSM8K 1B model: 15.2% → 39.0%.
